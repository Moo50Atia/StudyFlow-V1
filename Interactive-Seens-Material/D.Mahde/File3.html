The Grand Misconception

Software code (e.g., `A + B`) creates the illusion that multi-bit binary addition occurs instantaneously across all bits. In hardware, a Ripple Carry Adder suffers from O(N) linear delay. The Most Significant Bit (far left) cannot know its final state until the physical carry signal ripples sequentially through every preceding logic block.
