Bloodstream
[High Cl⁻] 100 mM
Cytoplasm & Nucleus
[Low Cl⁻] 4-20 mM

Replication Halted

DNA Polymerase Blocked by 35° Kink

Mechanism of Action

Time evolution of Cisplatin from extracellular transit to its final DNA-distorting crosslink.

1

Cellular Entry

Neutral cisplatin crosses the lipid bilayer into the low-chloride environment of the cell.

2

Activation (Aquation)

Low intracellular Cl⁻ concentration causes a Chlorine ligand to be replaced by water, forming an active Monoaqua complex.

3

DNA Binding

The positively charged complex moves to the nucleus and binds to the N7 position of Guanine bases.

4

Structural Distortion

Formation of an intra-strand crosslink between adjacent Guanines forcibly bends the DNA helix by 35-40°.
