Lecture: Deviance in Generalized Linear Models
=================================================

--- Scene 1 Design (Sec1): Comparison – Deviance as Scaled Distance from Perfect Fit ---

LAYOUT:
- A vertical coordinate grid (SVG) with l(β) (log-likelihood) on the y-axis.
- Two peaks on the grid: saturated model peak l(y, ŷ) at the top (perfect fit) and fitted model peak l(y, μ̂) below.
- A discrepancy gauge (vertical bar between the peaks) showing the gap.
- A ϕ dispersion signal slider on the side that scales the gap.

ANIMATIONS:
- The coordinate grid draws its axes (SVG line animation).
- The saturated peak appears at the top with a golden crown icon and star burst animation — representing "perfect fit."
- The fitted model peak appears lower with a standard marker.
- The discrepancy gauge extends between them (CSS height animation) with tick marks counting the distance.
- As the ϕ slider adjusts, the gauge scales: higher ϕ = gauge value decreases (dispersion scaling), with CSS transition on the gauge label value.

INTERACTIVITY:
- The ϕ dispersion slider adjusts the deviance scaling in real-time: D = 2(l_saturated - l_fitted) / ϕ.
- User can drag the fitted model peak up/down to simulate better/worse fit; the discrepancy gauge updates.
- Clicking the saturated peak shows: "Saturated model: one parameter per observation, perfect fit."
- Clicking the gap gauge shows: "Deviance = RELATIVE measure — how far from perfect, not absolute goodness."
- A "Compare Two Models" button adds a second fitted peak for side-by-side deviance comparison.

COLOR PALETTE:
- Saturated peak in gold (#eab308) with crown icon. Fitted model peak in cyan (#06b6d4). Discrepancy gauge fill in rose gradient (#f43f5e → #fbbf24). ϕ slider in purple (#8b5cf6). Grid lines in subtle gray (#374151). Background (#020617).

VARIABLE NAMING:
- coordinateGrid_SVG, saturatedPeak_l_y_yHat, fittedPeak_l_y_muHat, discrepancyGauge, devianceValue_D, dispersionSlider_phi, scaledDeviance, saturatedModel, fittedModel, relativeGoodness, crownIcon, tickMarks.

TECHNOLOGY: HTML + CSS + JS + SVG. No 3D required.


--- Scene 2 Design (Sec2): Comparison – Deviance Distribution: Individual vs Grouped Data ---

LAYOUT:
- Left panel: individual binary data points scattered on a graph (SVG circles at 0/1).
- Right panel: aggregated grouped data blocks (larger rectangles with count labels).
- Bottom: a deviance signal line that transforms from jagged (individual) to smooth (grouped).
- A χ²(G-p-1) distribution curve (SVG) that the smooth signal converges to.

ANIMATIONS:
- Individual data points appear scattered on the left panel.
- A "Group" transformation wave sweeps left-to-right: individual points cluster together, merge, and transform into grouped blocks on the right panel (CSS scale-down + translateX of individuals, scale-up + appearance of groups).
- The deviance signal below: initially jagged and erratic (SVG polyline with many sharp angles), smooths out as grouping occurs (SVG path d-attribute interpolation via JS to reduce vertices).
- The smoothed signal overlays onto the χ² curve, aligning with it. A "Valid ✓" badge appears for grouped, "Invalid ✗" for individual.

INTERACTIVITY:
- A "Group / Ungroup" toggle switches between individual and grouped views with full animation.
- A group size slider adjusts how many individuals merge per group (larger groups = smoother signal).
- Hovering over the jagged signal shows: "Deviance NOT χ² distributed for individual binary data."
- Hovering over the smooth signal shows: "Deviance ≈ χ²(G-p-1) for grouped data."
- Clicking the χ² curve shows its degrees of freedom calculation.

COLOR PALETTE:
- Individual data points in scattered colors (teal #14b8a6 for y=1, gray #6b7280 for y=0). Grouped blocks in deep blue (#1d4ed8). Jagged signal in red (#ef4444). Smooth signal in green (#22c55e). χ² curve in white (#e2e8f0). Valid badge green, Invalid badge red. Background (#0f172a).

VARIABLE NAMING:
- individualDataPoints_SVG, groupedDataBlocks, groupTransformationWave, devianceSignal_jagged, devianceSignal_smooth, chiSquaredCurve_G_p_1, groupToggle, groupSizeSlider, validBadge, invalidBadge, degreesOfFreedom_G_minus_p_minus_1, binaryData_individual, aggregatedData_grouped.

TECHNOLOGY: HTML + CSS + JS + SVG. No 3D required.


--- Scene 3 Design (Sec3): Accumulation – Pearson χ² Statistic Assembly ---

LAYOUT:
- Left: rows of observed (yᵢ) and fitted (μ̂ᵢ) value pairs as labeled blocks.
- Center: a squared difference computation zone where (yᵢ − μ̂ᵢ)² is calculated.
- A variance function filter v(μ̂ᵢ) (mesh/grid styled div) that each squared difference passes through.
- A summation accumulator (χ² bucket container) on the right.
- Final Pearson χ² value display.

ANIMATIONS:
- For each observation pair:
  1. yᵢ and μ̂ᵢ blocks slide toward each other, collide (CSS translateX converge + flash), producing a (yᵢ − μ̂ᵢ)² block (new div pops in with the squared value).
  2. The squared block moves downward through the v(μ̂ᵢ) filter (CSS translateY). The filter visually "weighs" it (block size adjusts based on v(μ̂ᵢ) value — larger variance = smaller weighted residual).
  3. The weighted block slides right into the summation bucket (CSS translateX + opacity fade into the bucket fill level).
- Bucket fill level rises with each added residual (CSS height transition).
- After all observations are processed, the bucket transforms into the Pearson χ² value with a glow.

INTERACTIVITY:
- "Process Next" button handles one observation at a time.
- "Process All" button runs the full sequence automatically.
- Hovering over the filter for each observation shows v(μ̂ᵢ) value and the weighting applied.
- A side panel compares Pearson χ² with Deviance: "Equivalent in large samples."
- Clicking the final χ² value shows the degrees of freedom and distributional reference.

COLOR PALETTE:
- Observed yᵢ in teal (#14b8a6). Fitted μ̂ᵢ in violet (#8b5cf6). Squared residual in amber (#f59e0b). Variance filter in gray mesh. Weighted residual in cyan (#06b6d4). Bucket fill in gradient green (#22c55e). Final χ² in gold (#eab308). Background (#0f172a).

VARIABLE NAMING:
- observedValue_y_i, fittedValue_muHat_i, squaredDifference_y_minus_muHat_sq, varianceFunctionFilter_v_muHat_i, weightedResidual, summationBucket_chiSquared, pearsonChiSquared_X2, bucketFillLevel, processNextButton, processAllButton, degreesOfFreedom, largesampleEquivalence.

TECHNOLOGY: HTML + CSS + JS. No 3D required.


--- Scene 4 Design (Sec4): Parallel Flow – Deviance Partitioning for Model Comparison ---

LAYOUT:
- A horizontal bar representing total deviance D(M̃) of the restricted model (full width).
- The bar splits into two segments: D(M) (complex model deviance) and D(M̃|M) (difference/reduction).
- The difference segment D(M̃|M) transforms into a log-likelihood ratio statistic.
- A χ²ᵣ distribution target curve (SVG) on the right.

ANIMATIONS:
- The full deviance bar D(M̃) appears at full width (CSS width animation from 0 to 100%).
- A vertical split line appears and animates across the bar, dividing it into D(M) on the left and D(M̃|M) on the right (CSS border + width transition).
- The D(M̃|M) segment detaches and floats upward (CSS translateY + slight rotation).
- The detached segment transforms into a styled "Log-Likelihood Ratio" badge (CSS shape morph via border-radius + background-color change).
- The badge slides rightward toward the χ²ᵣ distribution curve and docks at the test statistic position.
- If the statistic exceeds the critical value, a "REJECT: Models significantly different" badge appears.

INTERACTIVITY:
- A slider adjusts the relative sizes of D(M) and D(M̃|M) to show how adding predictors reduces deviance.
- Clicking D(M) shows: "Deviance of the more complex model."
- Clicking D(M̃|M) shows: "Improvement from adding predictors — this is what we test."
- A "Critical Value" line toggle on the χ² curve shows the rejection boundary.
- A dropdown selects different model comparisons (e.g., Null vs Full, Reduced vs Full).

COLOR PALETTE:
- Full deviance bar D(M̃) in rose (#f43f5e). Complex model D(M) in cyan (#06b6d4). Difference D(M̃|M) in amber (#f59e0b). Log-LR badge in gold (#eab308). χ²ᵣ curve in white. Critical value line in red dashed. Background (#020617).

VARIABLE NAMING:
- totalDeviance_D_Mtilde, complexModelDeviance_D_M, devianceDifference_D_Mtilde_given_M, splitLine, logLikelihoodRatioBadge, chiSquaredTarget_r, criticalValueLine, modelComparisonDropdown, rejectBadge, devianceBar_full, deviancePartition, improvementTest.

TECHNOLOGY: HTML + CSS + JS + SVG. No 3D required.
