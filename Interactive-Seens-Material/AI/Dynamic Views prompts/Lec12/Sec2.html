Full Probability Density
f(yᵢ|θᵢ,ϕ) = exp[(yᵢθᵢ - b(θᵢ)) / a(ϕ) + c(yᵢ,ϕ)]
First Moment (Mean)
E(yᵢ) = μᵢ = h(ηᵢ)
Second Moment (Variance)
Var(yᵢ) = ϕ v(μᵢ)

Misconception Corrected

Quasi-likelihood relies purely on the first two moments (mean and variance). This corrects the misconception that variance and mean can only be defined through a formal probability density function. You do not need the full density f(y) to estimate parameters correctly.
