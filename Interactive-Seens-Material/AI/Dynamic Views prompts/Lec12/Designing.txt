Lecture: Quasi-likelihood and Generalized Estimation Equations (GEEs)
======================================================================

--- Scene 1 Design (Sec1): Comparison – Quasi-Likelihood as Distribution Repair Module ---

LAYOUT:
- A diagnostic dashboard on the left (styled as a dark panel with three warning indicator lights: "Distribution", "Link", "Covariates").
- A failing model icon in the center (styled div with an error state — red border, shake animation).
- A Quasi-Likelihood repair module on the right (styled as a tool/wrench card that can be dragged toward the model).

ANIMATIONS:
- The three warning lights illuminate sequentially (CSS @keyframes: off → amber → red). The "Distribution" light stays red while "Link" and "Covariates" dim to green (correctly specified).
- The model icon shakes continuously (CSS transform: translateX alternating ±4px) with a red pulsing border.
- The Quasi-Likelihood module slides toward the model (CSS translateX) and docks onto the "Distribution" light.
- Upon docking: the "Distribution" light fades from red to "bypassed" state (transparent/gray with strikethrough label "Not Required"). The rigid distribution shells around the model fade out (CSS opacity: 0).
- The model icon transforms from red error state to green active state (border-color + background transition + shake stops).

INTERACTIVITY:
- User drags the Quasi-Likelihood module toward the dashboard (JS drag-and-drop). It only docks on the "Distribution" light — dragging to "Link" or "Covariates" lights shows a "Wrong Target" tooltip.
- Clicking each warning light shows what it checks: "Distribution: Is the assumed probability distribution correct?"
- A "Break Model" button re-activates the distribution error for replay.
- A comparison panel shows: "Full Likelihood: needs correct distribution. Quasi-Likelihood: needs only correct mean and variance structure."

COLOR PALETTE:
- Dashboard in dark charcoal (#1c1917). Warning lights: red (#ef4444), amber (#f59e0b), green (#22c55e). Failing model red glow. Quasi-Likelihood module in bright teal (#14b8a6) with wrench icon. Repaired model green glow. Background (#0c0a09).

VARIABLE NAMING:
- diagnosticDashboard, warningLight_Distribution, warningLight_Link, warningLight_Covariates, failingModelIcon, quasiLikelihoodModule, dockingTarget, distributionShells, repairedModelState, errorState_shake, greenActiveState, dragDropHandler, breakModelButton.

TECHNOLOGY: HTML + CSS + JS with Drag-and-Drop API. No 3D required.


--- Scene 2 Design (Sec2): Process – Moment-Based Specification (Mean & Variance Only) ---

LAYOUT:
- Left side: a complex probability density formula block (styled math div with full PDF notation — many terms, integrals, etc.).
- Center: a transformation processor (styled div with a "decompose" icon/gears).
- Right side: two primary signal blocks — "E(yᵢ) = μᵢ = h(ηᵢ)" (mean block) and "Var(yᵢ) = ϕ·v(μᵢ)" (variance block).
- The original formula area fades away.

ANIMATIONS:
- The complex PDF formula block sits prominently on the left, fully detailed and visually dense.
- It slides into the transformation processor (CSS translateX). Gears animate inside (CSS rotate).
- The processor splits the formula into two clean blocks that emerge on the right:
  - Mean block slides out top-right with a clean fade-in.
  - Variance block slides out bottom-right with a clean fade-in.
- The original PDF formula fades to opacity: 0.1 (ghost state) while the two moment blocks highlight with bright borders and glow.
- A dividing label "No full distribution needed — only moments" appears between the faded PDF and the bright blocks.

INTERACTIVITY:
- Clicking the original PDF shows: "Traditional approach: specify the entire probability distribution."
- Clicking the Mean block shows the response function h(ηᵢ) and how it maps the linear predictor to the mean.
- Clicking the Variance block shows the variance function v(μᵢ) examples: Poisson → v(μ) = μ, Gaussian → v(μ) = 1.
- A "Restore Full Distribution" button reverses the animation.
- A dropdown selects different variance functions to see how the variance block changes.

COLOR PALETTE:
- Complex PDF in muted gray/silver (indicating it will be discarded). Mean block in bright cyan (#06b6d4) with blue border. Variance block in bright amber (#f59e0b) with gold border. Processor in metallic gray. Faded PDF ghost in transparent white (rgba(255,255,255,0.1)). Background (#0f172a).

VARIABLE NAMING:
- complexPDF_formula, transformationProcessor, meanBlock_E_yi_mu, varianceBlock_Var_yi_phi_v, responseFunction_h_eta, varianceFunctionv_mu, dispersionParameter_phi, momentBasedSpecification, fullDistribution_ghost, restoreButton, varianceFunctionDropdown.

TECHNOLOGY: HTML + CSS + JS. No 3D required.


--- Scene 3 Design (Sec3): Comparison – ML vs Quasi-ML Estimator Efficiency ---

LAYOUT:
- Two parallel horizontal paths toward a true β target line (SVG vertical line on the right).
- Path A (top): β̂_ML signal traveling toward the target.
- Path B (bottom): β̂_QML signal traveling toward the target.
- Above the paths: two normal distribution curves centered on the target — one narrow (ML), one wider (QML).
- Labels: "Consistent ✓" on both, "Efficient ✓" only on ML.

ANIMATIONS:
- Both signals travel from left toward the true β target simultaneously (CSS translateX).
- Both arrive at the target (they're both consistent — unbiased), triggering arrival checkmarks.
- The distribution curves above animate: ML curve draws itself as a tall narrow spike (SVG path with tight standard deviation), QML curve draws as a shorter wider wave (SVG path with larger standard deviation).
- Efficiency comparison: an "Efficiency Gap" measurement bracket appears between the two curve widths with a label showing the relative efficiency.
- Both "Consistent ✓" labels appear with green checkmarks. The "Efficient ✓" label appears only on ML; QML gets "Less Efficient ~" in amber.

INTERACTIVITY:
- A "Sample Size" slider: as n increases, both curves narrow (but ML stays narrower). This demonstrates both are consistent but ML is more efficient.
- Clicking the ML curve shows: "Uses full distributional information — maximum efficiency."
- Clicking the QML curve shows: "Uses only moments — consistent but wider confidence intervals."
- A "Overlap" toggle superimposes both curves for direct comparison.
- A numeric display shows the asymptotic relative efficiency (ARE) ratio.

COLOR PALETTE:
- ML signal and curve in emerald (#10b981). QML signal and curve in amber (#f59e0b). True β target in white (#f8fafc) dashed line. Consistent checkmarks in green. Efficiency labels: green for ML, amber for QML. Background (#020617).

VARIABLE NAMING:
- estimatorPath_ML, estimatorPath_QML, trueBeta_targetLine, betaHat_ML_signal, betaHat_QML_signal, distributionCurve_ML_narrow, distributionCurve_QML_wide, consistencyLabel, efficiencyLabel, sampleSizeSlider, efficiencyGap_bracket, asymptoticRelativeEfficiency_ARE, overlapToggle.

TECHNOLOGY: HTML + CSS + JS + SVG. No 3D required.


--- Scene 4 Design (Sec4): Accumulation – Sandwich Estimator Construction ---

LAYOUT:
- Center: a "Working Variance" block V (large styled div with a subtle warning icon indicating potential misspecification).
- Left and Right: two robust information blocks F_Q⁻¹ approaching from both sides.
- The assembly forms a "bread-meat-bread" sandwich structure.

*** CONSIDER 3D FOR SANDWICH VISUAL ***
- Use CSS 3D transforms (perspective, translateZ, rotateX) to create a layered sandwich structure:
  - Bottom bread layer: F_Q⁻¹ left (translateZ(-20px))
  - Middle meat layer: V working variance (translateZ(0))
  - Top bread layer: F_Q⁻¹ right (translateZ(20px))
- Slight rotateX tilt on the parent container to show depth.

ANIMATIONS:
- The central V block sits in the middle, pulsing with a warning amber glow (CSS box-shadow animation) to indicate "potentially wrong variance."
- From the left: the first F_Q⁻¹ block slides in (CSS translateX + translateZ) and positions itself below V as the bottom "bread."
- From the right: the second F_Q⁻¹ block slides in and positions above V as the top "bread."
- The three layers compress together (CSS translateZ converging) to form the sandwich structure.
- A label "Sandwich Estimator: F_Q⁻¹ · V · F_Q⁻¹" materializes above the completed sandwich.
- The warning glow on V transforms from amber to green: "Robust despite misspecification."

ANIMATIONS (3D):
- Parent container has perspective: 800px and a slight rotateX(10deg) tilt for depth view.
- Layers slide in with translateZ depth offsets, then compress to form the sandwich.
- A rotation animation slowly rotates the completed sandwich on the Y-axis (rotateY 0° → 15° → -15° → 0°) to show the 3D layered structure.

INTERACTIVITY:
- A "Build Sandwich" button triggers the assembly sequence step by step.
- Clicking V shows: "Working Variance — may be incorrectly specified, but sandwich corrects for this."
- Clicking either F_Q⁻¹ block shows: "Robust (empirical) information matrix — provides correction."
- A "Rotate View" control lets the user spin the sandwich to see all layers.
- A comparison toggle shows "Naive Variance (just V)" vs "Sandwich Variance (F⁻¹VF⁻¹)" — the naive one flashes red, the sandwich glows green.

COLOR PALETTE:
- V working variance block in amber (#f59e0b) with warning state, transitioning to green (#22c55e) when sandwiched. F_Q⁻¹ blocks in deep blue (#1d4ed8) representing robustness. Sandwich assembled state has a golden border (#eab308). Label in white. Background (#0c0a09).

VARIABLE NAMING:
