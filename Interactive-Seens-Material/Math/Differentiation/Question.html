The Sliding Ladder

A classic related rates calculus problem. A 10m ladder is leaning against a wall. The bottom is pulled away at a constant rate of 1 m/s. Observe how the downward speed of the top increases non-linearly as it falls.

Time: 0.00s

Derivation Calculus In Action

1. Pythagorean Theorem

x² + y² = L²

2. Differentiate with respect to Time (t)

2x(dx/dt) + 2y(dy/dt) = 0

3. Isolate the falling rate (dy/dt)

dy/dt = -
x y
· dx/dt
