Transformation Rigor: $y(t) = T\{x(t)\}$ Mathematical Map

Core Signal proof
Proof Formulation T{x(t)} = x²(t)

Let $x_1(t)$ and $x_2(t)$ be inputs. The output to an arbitrary linear combination is:
$T\{a \cdot x_1(t) + b \cdot x_2(t)\} = [a \cdot x_1(t) + b \cdot x_2(t)]^2$ But scaling the individual outputs yields:
$a \cdot T\{x_1(t)\} + b \cdot T\{x_2(t)\} = a \cdot x_1^2(t) + b \cdot x_2^2(t)$ Since they are unequal for general signals, the system is strictly Non-linear.

Classifications Cheat Sheet:

Linearity Satisfies Superposition Principle: $T\{a \cdot x_1 + b \cdot x_2\} = a \cdot T\{x_1\} + b \cdot T\{x_2\}$.
Time Invariance An input delay produces output delay only: $y(t - \tau) = T\{x(t - \tau)\}$.
Memory vs Memoryless Output depends purely on input at current instant, with no reliance on past/future inputs.
Causality Does not rely on future outputs or inputs ($t_{future}$). Always causal in realistic physical boards.
