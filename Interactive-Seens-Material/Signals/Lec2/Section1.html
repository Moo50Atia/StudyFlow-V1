Mathematical Model

A discrete-time unit step signal $u[n]$ can be synthesized by accumulating shifted unit impulses $\delta[n-k]$:

Moving the slider positions a single impulse at index k. By sweeping k from $0$ to $\infty$, you stamp impulses that assemble into a solid wall of height $1.0$ representing $u[n]$.

The impulse's **Sifting (Sampling)** property selects the exact amplitude of a wave $x[n]$ at a specified time step $k$:

Multiplying a continuous/complex discrete-time wave by a shifted unit impulse $\delta[n-k]$ eliminates all points except at index $k$.

Signal Glossary

The impulse $\delta[n]$ is the atom of digital signal processing. Every discrete signal is a sum of scaled, shifted impulses. This fundamental concept allows us to construct convolutions and analyze LTI systems.
