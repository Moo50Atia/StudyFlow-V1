Inverse Z-Transform

Polynomial Long Division

The inverse Z-transform can be found by expanding \(X(z)\) into a power series. The sequence values x[n] emerge directly as the coefficients of this division.

The Crucial Misconception:

Division order is not independent of the Region of Convergence (ROC). The way we order the divisor determines the direction of the resulting sequence in the time domain.
