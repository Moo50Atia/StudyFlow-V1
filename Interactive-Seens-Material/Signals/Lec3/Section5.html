Understanding the Convolution Algorithm

F

Flip: why do we reverse h[k]?

The mathematical operation uses $h[n-k]$. In terms of variable $k$, the expression $-k$ reverses the direction of the impulse response, turning left-to-right spikes into a right-to-left profile.

S

Shift: slide across k-axis

As the output index $n$ moves from left to right, the flipped signal $h[n-k]$ shifts rightward. This interaction scans the input sequence step-by-step, mimicking the dynamic signal flow of a physical system.

M

Multiply-Accumulate

At each discrete location, we multiply values sharing the same vertical coordinate index $k$, and sum the resulting products. The total represents a single discrete output stem value $y[n]$.
