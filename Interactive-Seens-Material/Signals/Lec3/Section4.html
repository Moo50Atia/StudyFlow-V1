💡 Misconception Buster: Why is the output not a rectangle?

A common misconception in linear system theory is that "the convolution of two rectangular pulses must result in another rectangle." It does not.

Because convolution represents the accumulated sliding area of overlap between the signals, the process is inherently continuous:

1. Collision Entry The overlap area accumulates linearly, forming a ramp.
2. Complete Insertion The overlap remains constant, forming a flat-top plateau.
3. Exit Process The overlap drops linearly, forming a decay ramp.
