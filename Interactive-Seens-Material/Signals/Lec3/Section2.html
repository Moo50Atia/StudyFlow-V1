Understanding Convolution Graphically

Convolution measures the overlap area of two functions as one passes over the other. Let's trace the steps visualized in this simulation:

  1. Flip $h(\tau) \rightarrow h(-\tau)$: We reflect the impulse response signal horizontally around the origin.
  2. Shift to get $h(t - \tau)$: As you drag the slider, you shift the flipped signal along the horizontal axis by time $t$.
  3. Multiply $x(\tau) \cdot h(t - \tau)$: At every point, we multiply the fixed blue signal $x(\tau)$ with the moving amber signal. The result is plotted in the middle panel.
  4. Integrate (Accumulate Area): The area under the product curve (filled in purple) is calculated and mapped directly to the bottom green curve $y(t)$ at the current position $t$.
