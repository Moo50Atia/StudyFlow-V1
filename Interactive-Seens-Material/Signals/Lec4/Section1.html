Misconception Buster: Multiplication vs. Convolution

A highly common trap is assuming that system convolution is simply the term-by-term (pointwise) product of their discrete sequences: y[n] = x[n] · h[n]. In reality, LTI systems have memory. Convolution combines the components using delayed and scaled impulses: y[n] = x[n] * h[n].

True Convolution (x * h) Spreading, Delayed Memory
System Memory: The output spreads in time (length is L_x + L_h - 1 = 7 samples). Signals overlap continuously as time advances.
Pointwise Multiplication (x • h) Instantaneous, No Memory
Instantaneous Mask: Terms are multiplied only at matching coordinates. It does not model time delays (length is capped at 5 samples, and is mostly zero).

Mathematical Truths: Why Term-by-Term Fails

1

Linear Time-Invariant Behavior

LTI system components are completely defined by their response to a single impulse ($h[n]$). If an input is a sum of delayed impulses ($x[k]\delta[n-k]$), the output must be the sum of delayed responses. This forces the time-shift summation sequence.

2

Pointwise assumes "Memoryless"

Doing $x[n] \cdot h[n]$ implies that what happens at $n=2$ only depends on input at $n=2$. But real physical filters, audio echoes, and transmission lines have capacitors or delay paths that carry energy forward.

3

Sequence Output Dimensions

Convolution mathematically extends the sequence. If an audio track (duration 5s) runs through a speaker reverberation filter (duration 3s), the resulting sound fades out over 7s. Direct multiplication would clip it at 5s!
