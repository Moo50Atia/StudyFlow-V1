Common LTI Misconception Buster

The False Belief:

"Connecting two linear systems in series simply adds their individual impulse responses together: $h_{eq}(t) = h_1(t) + h_2(t)$."

The Real Math:
For cascaded (Series) systems, the output of System 1 serves as the input to System 2. Therefore, they are combined using Convolution ($*$) rather than addition. Parallel systems are the ones that add!

Mathematical Formulation of LTI Interconnection

Series Cascaded Setup

When system blocks are aligned sequentially (output of system 1 becomes the input to system 2):

y(t) = [x(t) * h₁(t)] * h₂(t)
By associativity:
y(t) = x(t) * [h₁(t) * h₂(t)]

Hence, the equivalent system response is the Convolution of both responses: h_eq(t) = h₁(t) * h₂(t).

Parallel Summed Setup

When the input signal branches out simultaneously to multiple system blocks and their outputs are summed:

y(t) = y₁(t) + y₂(t)
y(t) = (x * h₁)(t) + (x * h₂)(t)
By distributivity:
y(t) = x(t) * [h₁(t) + h₂(t)]

Hence, the equivalent system response is the Sum of both responses: h_eq(t) = h₁(t) + h₂(t).

Frequency (s-Domain) View

Converting time-domain convolution into Laplace s-domain highlights why the misconception happens:

Series: H_eq(s) = H₁(s) × H₂(s)
Parallel: H_eq(s) = H₁(s) + H₂(s)

Students often confuse time-domain operations with s-domain operations. While Series systems multiply in frequency, they must convolve in time!
