The Dirac Delta is not a standard function, but a generalized distribution. It is defined strictly by its integral properties. Geometrically, the derivative of a jump discontinuity (like the unit step at t=0) produces an infinitely tall, infinitely narrow spike whose total area exactly matches the height of the jump.
