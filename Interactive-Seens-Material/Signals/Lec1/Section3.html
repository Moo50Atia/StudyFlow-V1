Signal Symmetry Lab Guide

Welcome to the Interactive Symmetry Lab! Signal processing uses mathematics to understand physical waves. This simulator visualizes these equations perfectly in 3D.

What you will learn:

  • Time-Reversal $x(-t)$: Flipping a signal horizontally makes time run backwards. Any point $(t, y)$ maps to $(-t, y)$.
  • Even Signals $x(-t) = x(t)$: These signals are perfectly symmetrical about the Y-axis. Folding the graph in half over the vertical mirror plane results in identical overlap.
  • Odd Signals $x(-t) = -x(t)$: These signals have rotational symmetry. Rotating the graph $180^\circ$ around the origin matches itself, or reflecting across time *and* amplitude matches itself.

Interact and Play:

Toggle between Continuous and Discrete modes. Use your mouse on the 3D viewport on the right to tilt the workspace to observe the spatial transformation pathway!
