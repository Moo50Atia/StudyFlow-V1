Definition of Periodicity

A signal is classified as periodic if and only if there exists a positive constant T > 0 (or integer N > 0) such that copying a segment of that width and shifting it by T yields a perfect match with the future waveform. If the alignment is ever less than 100%, the signal is strictly non-periodic.
