An algebraic X(s) represents multiple completely unique time signals. The mapping is only unique when the Region of Convergence (ROC) is defined.

•

Right-half plane ROCs match poles mapping to u(t). Left-half plane ROCs match poles mapping to -u(-t) (anti-causal).

The Multi-Mapping Phenomenon: 1 Algebra ⟶ 3 Distinct Signals

Notice how the single expression X(s) = 1/((s - p₁)(s - p₂)) translates to three entirely different real-world behaviors depending on where convergence is assumed on the s-plane:
